Plant-by-plant: uses & quick recipes

Chamomile — calming tea

Chamomile soothes digestion and supports restful sleep. Quick recipe: steep 1 tsp dried flowers in hot water for 8-10 minutes. Drink before bed.

Yarrow — inflammation & wound care

Yarrow has a long history as a first-aid herb for minor wounds and inflammation. Try: make a simple infused oil and turn into a salve for cuts and scrapes.

Lavender — sleep & calm

Lavender is calming and aromatic. Try: sachets for sleep, or a gentle infused oil for massage.

Echinacea — immune support

Echinacea supports short-term immune function. Try: make a short tincture protocol during early cold symptoms.

Calendula — skin healing

Calendula is excellent for skin irritations and rashes. Try: calendula-infused oil made into a nourishing salve.

Peppermint — digestion

Peppermint relieves mild digestive discomfort. Try: fresh leaf tea or a cooling poultice for headaches.
